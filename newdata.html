Data Stories about World Happiness Report 2015-2022!

Data Set Description:

World Happiness Report 2015-2022: The World Happiness Report is a publication of the United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network. It contains articles and rankings of national happiness, based on respondent ratings of their own lives, which the report also correlates with various (quality of) life factors. As of March 2022, Finland had been ranked the happiest country in the world five times in a row.

The report primarily uses data from the Gallup World Poll. Each annual report is available to the public to download on the World Happiness Report website. The Editors of the 2020 report are John F. Helliwell, Richard Layard, Jeffrey D. Sachs, and Jan-Emmanuel De Neve. Associate Editors are Lara Aknin, Shun Wang, and Haifang Huang.

Here is the link to the original Data Set.

For the visualization below, I tried to explore

1: Countries from regions have a higher Happiness score and

2: Which factors seem to be most related to Happiness Scores.

I use a subset of instead of the entire data set. The subset includes 470 observations and 14 variables.

A graph illustrates part of the data set that I am using is attached below.

The graphs below will mainly focus on the following variables:

Quantitative Variables: Happiness Score, GDP Per Capita, Freedom Index, Health Life Expectancy;

Categorical Variables: Region, Happiness Score Categories.

The descriptions for the variables are detached below and can be found from this link.

-Happiness score or subjective well-being (SWB): The survey measure of SWB is from the December 26, 2014 release of the Gallup World Poll (GWP), which covers the years from 2005 to 2014. Unless stated otherwise, it is the national average response to the question of life evaluations.

-The statistics of GDP per capita in purchasing power parity (PPP) at constant 2011 international dollar prices are from the November 6, 2014 release of the World Development Indicators (WDI).

-Corruption Perception: The measure is the national average of the survey re- sponses to two questions in the Gallup World Poll: “Is corruption widespread throughout the government or not” and “Is corruption widespread within busi- nesses or not?”

-Healthy Life Expectancy (HLE). The time series of healthy life expectancy at birth are calculated by the authors based on data from the World Health Or- ganization (WHO), the World Development Indicators (WDI), and statistics published in journal articles.

-Freedom to make life choices is the national average of responses to the question “Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?”

-The variable Happiness Categories is the variable Happiness Scores divide into three categories: low (0-33%: 2.39-4.794), medium (34%-66%: 4.794-5.892), and high (67%-100%: 5.892-7.587).
